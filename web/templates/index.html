The 7 Deadly Sins of Software Agility.

Poor Committment to Process

The #1 cause of a failed adapatation to Agile processes is not committing to them. One of the most dangerous myths surrounding Agile is that it is highly customizable to your business needs. It isn't. Agile is fragile. The core elements that make up an Agile framework are so heavily intertwined and interdependent that relaxing or outright removing one process can have a ripple effect across the board.

Poor Team Stability

There are few things as damaging to your velocity as high team turn over. Agility depends on well functioning teams that have taken the time to gel. Everytime you move someone between teams, or hire, or fire someone from the team you will experience a nose dive in programmer productivity. This is especially true of startups who have high contractor and consultant turnover.

Poor Testing Habits

In the very early stages of a startup, investing in test driven development or well tested software can be a mistake. The point at this stage is to validate the business. That said, it is equally devistating to your business when you take that untested and unstable prototype and try to shove it into production. Throw that prototype in the trash! Building the same software from scratch in a test driven approach will net you huge gains (often a lot sooner than you think).

Blocking Code Reviews

At Blueshift we're huge fans of code review. We love it so much we review each other's code constantly and we pair together on solutions as many chances as we get. However, we often see code review being abused by teams to discuss in-depth the philosopy of the implementations. This does not lead to higher quality software and blocking reviews grind productivity to a halt.
